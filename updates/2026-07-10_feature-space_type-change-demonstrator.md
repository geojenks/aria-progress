First use of the map's coordinates to design an actuator, then verification by
simulating it: a bellows fitted with a deliberately slack spine tendon, predicted to
change behaviour type mid-stroke. The characterised device does exactly that — pure
axial extension at low pressure, then the tendon engages and the bend fraction of its
motion climbs from 0.1% to 22.2% over the sweep, a clear knee in its trajectory
through the deformation plane. Crucially, its blocked axial force curve is identical
to the plain bellows (both 4.11 N at 400 Pa: a blocked tip means the tendon never
engages), so a classical force–stroke chart cannot distinguish the two designs at all;
the deformation-composition axes separate them cleanly. The record uses the extension
branch of the sweep; the contraction branch needs a cleaner settling procedure before
it is included.
