A single figure presenting the McKibben braid-angle sweep explicitly as a validation:
the simulated axial response, extracted from geometry and materials alone, is overlaid
with the closed-form ideal-fibre prediction (sign index 1 − 3cos²θ). Both panels — free
stroke (A) and blocked axial force (B), two independent observables — reverse sign from
contraction to extension across the neutral angle θ* = arccos(1/√3) = 54.7°, which was
never encoded anywhere in the model. The 55° instance sits at the neutral angle and
barely moves (|stroke| ≈ 0.08 mm, ~0.14× the family's authority); its direction is
numerical floor noise, so it is flagged and read only as the near-zero null — which is
exactly the zero-crossing the theory predicts. Trend and sign validation only: absolute
magnitudes remain comparative pending calibration.
