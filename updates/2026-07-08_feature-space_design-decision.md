A worked demonstration that the capability map answers a designer's question, not just a
taxonomist's. An illustrative specification (free strain ≥ 10% of body length, blocked
force ≥ 0.25 N, ≤ 2 effective DOF) is shaded onto the Ashby-style log-log stroke–force
chart and graded per-record in a traceable requirement matrix: the accordion bellows is
the only outright pass; the thin/mid-wall pneu-nets are near-misses on force (7–17%
under); the contracting McKibbens trade force for stroke. The chart also verifies a
physical reading of the layout: the three contracting McKibbens lie on a common
iso-energy contour F·x ≈ 3.3 mJ (within ±25%, fitted slope −0.82 vs the ideal −1),
i.e. the braid-angle sweep trades stroke for force at roughly constant work capacity,
while the pneu-net wall-thickness family is instead iso-force (force spread ×1.32 vs
stroke ×2.06). Honest gaps stay visible: the cable finger is "incomplete" (no blocked
force measured), and the 55° McKibben is "not rated" (floor noise at the magic angle).
Thresholds are illustrative and simulated magnitudes remain comparative, not
predictive, pending calibration — the point is the machinery: every verdict traces to a
number already in the records.
